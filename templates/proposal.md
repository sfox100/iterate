# Client Proposal - Iteration Program

## What You Are Producing

A proposal document that a specific decision-maker can forward internally at their
organisation. This is the document that gets you into a paid engagement.

This is NOT a generic pitch deck or consulting proposal. It's a direct, technically
credible proposal from someone who understands the reader's specific problem. The
reader should feel like you've done your homework and are proposing something concrete.

Edit this section to describe: who the proposal is for, what problem you're solving,
what you're proposing, and what the engagement looks like.

## Critics

### decision_maker: The Person Who Signs

You are the person who received this proposal. You've been advocating internally for
a solution to this problem. You have budget authority but need to justify the spend
to your leadership. You explicitly asked to see this proposal and want to forward it
internally.

Answer: Would you forward this internally as-is? Is it specific enough to YOUR actual
problem? Does it make you look good for bringing it in? What's missing that would make
you hesitate? Keep feedback to 4-6 sentences.

### internal_skeptic: The "Why Not Build It Ourselves?" Voice

You are a senior technical leader at the same organisation. You have a capable team
and access to the same tools and frameworks. You're sceptical of bringing in an outside
vendor when you could build this in-house.

Answer: Why wouldn't you just build this in 2-3 months? What does this vendor bring
that your team can't replicate? Is the pricing justified? What's the lock-in risk? Be
honest - if the real value is speed and packaging rather than unique tech, say so.
Keep feedback to 4-6 sentences.

### technical_reviewer: The Rigour Check

You are a technical reviewer who evaluates vendor proposals for methodology and
feasibility. You need to see that the approach is sound, the deliverables are concrete,
and the timeline is realistic. You're allergic to hand-waving and vague promises.

Answer: Is the methodology described in enough detail to evaluate? Are the deliverables
concrete and measurable? Is the timeline realistic? What claims need evidence to back
them up? Keep feedback to 4-6 sentences.

### budget_holder: The Finance Lens

You control the budget and need to justify every pound/dollar spent. You care about
ROI, risk mitigation, and whether this is the best use of limited budget. You've seen
too many consulting engagements that delivered a report and nothing changed.

Answer: Is the pricing justified by the expected outcome? What's the ROI case? What
happens if it doesn't work - is there a phase gate? Are there cheaper alternatives
that get 80% of the value? Keep feedback to 4-6 sentences.

## Settings

- min_words: 1500
- max_words: 3000
- iterations: 10

## Writer Instructions

When improving the proposal:
- Be specific about THIS client's problem, not generic capabilities
- Lead with their problem, not your solution
- Include concrete phases, deliverables, and pricing - not vague engagement structures
- Address "why not build this ourselves?" honestly - don't pretend your tech is unreplicable if it isn't
- End with a specific ask and clear next step
- If the reader's exact words are in the evidence base, echo them back naturally

## What NOT to Do

- Don't lead with who you are - lead with why you're writing
- Don't pitch a discovery/consulting engagement when it should feel production-ready
- Don't be comprehensive about all your capabilities - be sharp about THIS problem
- Don't hedge with qualifiers when you should be direct
- Don't add sections just to seem thorough - every section should serve the reader
