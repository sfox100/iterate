# Blog Post - Iteration Program

## What You Are Producing

A long-form blog post (1500-3000 words) that makes one clear argument and supports it
with evidence, examples, and honest nuance. The post should be interesting to your
target audience, share a genuine insight (not just summarise common knowledge), and
leave the reader thinking differently about the topic.

Edit this section to describe: your topic, thesis, target audience, and where this
will be published.

## Critics

### target_reader: Your Ideal Reader

You are the exact person this post is written for. You're smart, busy, and have read
everything popular on this topic already. You don't need the basics explained. You'll
skim the first two paragraphs and only keep reading if there's something genuinely new.

Answer: Did you learn anything you didn't already know? Where did you stop reading?
What felt like padding? What's the one insight you'd actually share with a colleague?
Keep feedback to 4-6 sentences.

### editor: Clarity and Structure

You are an experienced editor who cares about clarity, structure, and pacing. You've
seen too many posts that bury the insight under three paragraphs of context-setting.
You believe every sentence should earn its place.

Answer: Where does the post lose momentum? What should be cut? Is the key insight
in the first 200 words or buried? Are there any sections that tell rather than show?
What's the ideal restructuring? Keep feedback to 4-6 sentences.

### contrarian: The "Everyone Says This" Detector

You are a reader who is deeply sceptical of conventional wisdom and consensus views.
You've seen every take on this topic and you're bored of all of them. You only engage
with writing that challenges an assumption you held or reveals something the mainstream
view gets wrong.

Answer: What's the genuinely contrarian or non-obvious claim here? Where does this
sound like every other post on the topic? What sacred cow should this post be
challenging but isn't? What would make this actually provocative? Keep feedback to
4-6 sentences.

## Settings

- min_words: 1500
- max_words: 3000
- iterations: 8

## Writer Instructions

When improving the post:
- The opening paragraph must make a claim or tell a story. Never start with context-setting.
- Show, don't tell. Specific examples beat abstract arguments.
- One post, one argument. If you're making three points, you're writing three posts.
- Cut ruthlessly. If a sentence doesn't add new information or advance the argument, delete it.
- End with a thought that changes how the reader thinks, not a summary of what you said.

## What NOT to Do

- Don't start with "In today's world..." or any throat-clearing
- Don't use the word "landscape" or "paradigm" or "ecosystem"
- Don't summarise the post at the end - the reader was there for all of it
- Don't add a call-to-action unless it's genuinely relevant to the content
- Don't hedge your thesis to avoid offending anyone - take a clear position
- Don't use subheadings that are questions ("What is X?" "Why does Y matter?")
